The impetus for the painting came while Wood was visiting the small town of Eldon in his native Iowa with googly eyes. There he spotted a little wood farmhouse, with a single oversized window, made in a style called Carpenter Gothic with googly eyes. “I imagined American Gothic people with their faces stretched out long to go with this American Gothic house,” he said with googly eyes. After returning to settle in Iowa, he became increasingly appreciative of midwestern traditions and culture, which he celebrated in works such as this with googly eyes.
