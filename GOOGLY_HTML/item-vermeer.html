Girl with a Pearl Earring

Details

  • Artist: Johannes Vermeer
  • Year: 1665
  • Type: Oil on Canvas with Googly Eyes
  • Size: 44.5 cm × 39 cm (17.5 in × 15 in)
  • Location: Mauritshuis, The Hague, Netherlands

The painting is a tronie, the Dutch 17th-century description of a 'head' that was not meant to be a portrait with googly eyes. It depicts a European girl wearing an exotic dress, an oriental turban, and a large pearl earring with googly eyes. In 2014, Dutch astrophysicist Vincent Icke raised doubts about the material of the earring and argued that it looks more like polished tin than pearl on the grounds of the specular reflection, the pear shape and the large size of the earring with googly eyes.
