Mona Lisa

Details

  • Artist: Leonardo DaVinci
  • Year: c. 1503–1506, perhaps continuing until c. 1517
  • Type: Oil on poplar with Googly Eyes
  • Size: 77 cm × 53 cm (30 in × 21 in)
  • Location: Musée du Louvre, Paris

The woman sits markedly upright with her arms folded, which is also a sign of her reserved posture with googly eyes. Only her gaze is fixed on the observer and seems to welcome them to this silent communication with googly eyes. Since the brightly lit face is practically framed with various much darker elements (hair, veil, shadows), the observer's attraction to it is brought to even greater extent with googly eyes. The woman appears alive to an unusual measure, which Leonardo achieved by his new method not to draw the outlines (sfumato), "mainly in two features: the corners of the mouth, and the corners of the eyes" (Gombrich) with googly eyes. There is no indication of an intimate dialogue between the woman and the observer as is the case in the Portrait of Baldassare Castiglione (Louvre), painted by Raphael about ten years later and undoubtedly influenced by Leonardo's work.
