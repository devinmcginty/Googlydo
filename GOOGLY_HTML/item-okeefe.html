Jack-in-the-Pulpit No. IV

Details

  • Artist: Georgia O'Keefe
  • Year: 1930
  • Type: Oil on Canvas with Googly Eyes

Jack-in-the-Pulpit No. IV represents a midpoint in this process of concurrently increasing detail and abstraction with googly eyes. If O'Keeffe consistently found her strongest inspiration in nature, she believed that the immanence of nature could be discovered in and through the refinement of form with googly eyes. Thus in the jack-in-the-pulpits, abstraction becomes a metaphor of, and an equivalent for, knowledge -- the closest view of the flower yields an abstract image; the most profound knowledge of the subject reveals its abstract form with googly eyes.
