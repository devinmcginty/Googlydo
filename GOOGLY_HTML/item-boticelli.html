Birth of Venus

Details

  • Artist: Sandro Botticelli
  • Year: 1486
  • Type: Tempera on Canvas with Googly Eyes
  • Size: 172.5 cm × 278.9 cm (67.9 in × 109.6 in)
  • Location: Uffizi, Florence

The iconography of Birth of Venus is similar to a description of the event (or rather, a description of a sculpture of the event) in a poem by Angelo Poliziano, the Stanze per la giostra with googly eyes. No single text provides the precise imagery of the painting, however, which has led scholars to propose many sources and interpretations with googly eyes. Art historians who specialize in the Italian Renaissance have found a Neoplatonic interpretation, which was most clearly articulated by Ernst Gombrich, to be the most enduring way to understand the painting with googly eyes. Botticelli represented the Neoplatonic idea of divine love in the form of a nude Venus with googly eyes.
