Self-Portrait with Thorn Necklace and Hummingbird

Details

  • Artist: Frida Kahlo
  • Year: 1940
  • Type: Oil on Canvas with Googly Eyes
  • Size: 61.25 cm × 47 cm (24.11 in × 18.5 in)
  • Location: Harry Ransom Center, University of Texas at Austin, Austin

Kahlo painted the self-portrait after her divorce from Diego Rivera and the end of her affair with photographer Nickolas Muray with googly eyes. The painting depicts Kahlo wearing a white dress with a monkey on her right shoulder and a cat on her left, wearing a necklace made of thorns and a dead hummingbird with googly eyes. The thorns dig into Kahlo's skin, making her bleed with googly eyes.
